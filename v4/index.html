Why Aerial

The power-user version of the screensaver Apple ships.

macOS already plays a few aerial videos out of the box — and they look great. But the built-in version stops there: a small fixed library, no overlays, no multi-display tricks, no community packs, no live wallpaper, no way to drop in your own footage.

Aerial picks up where Apple stopped. The full Apple library at native quality (the new 4K 240fps set included), your own videos in the rotation, live camera feeds, drag-and-drop overlays, per-monitor playback, global hotkeys for the wallpaper, and a curated catalog of community-made expansions — all in a single small .app that auto-updates itself.

Free, open source, no telemetry. Donations welcome but never asked twice.

Bring your own videos

Drop any `.mov` or `.mp4` into a single folder and Aerial picks it up — title it, tag it, hide it, send it to a playlist.

Since 2015

Old project. New tricks.

Aerial was the first to bring the Apple TV aerials to the Mac, almost a decade before Apple shipped a watered-down version of their own. It's still the most flexible, most feature-rich way to use them — and Aerial 4 is its biggest leap yet: 4K 240fps slowed for the desktop with smart pause-on-occlusion, live camera feeds, your own videos in the rotation, global hotkeys for the wallpaper, a brand-new visual overlay editor, and the new App Extension screensaver API for perfect modern macOS compatibility.
